BindingBooks

Welcome to my showcase for our Django Web Development Group Project 2025

The assignment was exceptionally open ended. In a team of 4-8 people, create a website. About what? Whatever we wanted. The main requirement was to work together using scrum and to use django in a more complex way than the class had used up to that point. We decided to do a forum website for books. We wanted a lot of things from our website. We wanted to be able to purchase e-books, have dedicated forums, profiles. we wanted discussions to feel live, and have a distinct meeting time. We wanted moderation and the ability to search, and we wanted to be able to review books. Our sights were set high, and we accomplished most of our less important goals, and all of our major ones. Individually, we all had certain tasks and sections to take care of. Mine was the forums.

The lessons I learned

Throughout the process of creating BindingBooks, we learned a lot. One of the more important things we learned, in my opinion, is to not save the look of the site until the end. We had mostly postponed adding decoration until the final 2 weeks of development, and it ended up being a bit of a headache to go back through and try to integrate the bootstrap theme with everything we had already accomplished. For a few of us, it replaced some of our work, which was a bit dissapointing, but overall it made the site look much nicer and put together so it was the right call. I learned quite about about scrum, and since we switched off the scrum master position every week, most of us got a chance to steer the ship. Working within sprints was also new to me, and, with the exception of our bootstrap snafu, the sprints didn't break previous work, which was a nice way to get work done. I gained some additional experience with django, and with stripe. Overall, I learned a good amount and enjoyed working with my team.
